70s Visual Culture



Make Me Up; Cosmetics Advertisements

These advertisements highlight the way Women were presented to the world in the 1970s. At the time second-wave feminism was blooming and it's interesting to look at ads like these in order to see the capitalist idea of a Woman. Each ad obviously has a level of fantasy to it, but they still represent an idea of how a woman was or was idealized in the seventies.

From a graphic design perspective, the idea of clean design was still very much present then, but the visuals were equally as strong and full of color. I love how big and prevalent the type is as it's really fun and leaves almost no space for anything but the tagline.

This ad for Maybelline appeals to me mostly because of the product itself. The idea that they were able to fit so many products in one compact container is cool and innovative to me. Also in the tagline for the product it talks about this idea of a girl that travels and goes places. This concept which seems simple to us now, is so engrained in the design of the product and represents something much bigger. This represents the introduction of a time where it was majorly acceptable for a woman to be working and going places other than the home.
